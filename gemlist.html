January

Garnet

     Garnet is best known with the colour red though they may appear in other colours such as orange, purple, and green. Colours are not the only property that varies but there are also different types of garnets. For example, Almandine Garnet, Andradite Garnet, Color-Change Garnet, Demantoid Garnet, Grossularite Garnet, Hessonite Garnet, etc. There have healing effects and protective powers.







February

Amethyst

     Amethyst is a violet quartz that is adopted as one of the symbols of Ontario. The colour of amethyst range from purple to pale red-violet. The darker the colour the more valuable it is. The colour of some of them could be so light that it's almost colourless. It is said that amethyst promote peacefulness , happiness, and balance. It is also a good luck charm that protects one from thieves.





March

Aquamarine

     Aquamarine is a beautiful gemstone with a range of colour from from greenish blue to blue-green. Just like amethyst the darker the colour the more valuable it is. Dark blue amethysts and ones that produce a cat's eye or star effect are rare. One awsome property is that this stone has great transparency and clarity compared to the other stones. The word aquamarine consists of two words: aqua and marine. Both words relate to water. It is believed to protect the sailors.





April

Diamond

     The diamond is world's most popular gemstone. It is the hardest natural mineral and is chemically resistant. Diamonds have so many natural colours. They could be steel gray, white, blue, yellow, orange, red, green, a range of pink to purple, brown, and even black! Amongst all these colours guess which colour is the most expansive. Red Diamonds are the rarest so they are more costly and precious. Amazing right? People believe that diamond represents prosperity and perseverance.









May

Emerald

     Emerald, like many other gemstones, are judged on their colour. When the word emerald comes up people usually think of green. Yes, they are typically green, but their colour actually range from yellow-green to blue-green. The darker, the purer, and the more lively the green, the more valuable the emerald is. It is believed to be a symbol of rebirth and to have the power to grant the owner foresight, good fortune, and youth.















June

Pearl

     The pearl is the oldest known gem, and for many centuries it was considered the most valuable. Unlike all gems, the pearl is organic matter derived from a living creature - oysters and mollusks. Over time, the pearl has become the symbol of purity and innocence and it is often sewn into bridal gowns, or worn as jewelry by the bride.

Alexandrite

     Alexandrite is an extremely rare gemstone and a fairly modern one to boot. The story of the stone’s discovery goes like this: Miners were working alone in the mountains one day, collecting emeralds. One miner gathered some stones, which looked like emeralds and took them back to the camp at the end of the day. But in the light of the campfire, the stones shone a brilliant shade of red! The miners were perplexed. When morning came and they saw that the stones were green again in the light, they realized that they had found a new and mysterious gem.

Ruby

     The fiery and captivating Ruby is a stone of nobility, considered the most magnificent of all gems. It is the queen of stones and the stone of kings. Ruby is a stone that is considered in crystal healing to be very protective of home, worldly possession, children, as well as psychic protection. It is also said to protect against unhappiness and lightning. It is harder than any natural gemstone except diamond, which means a ruby is durable enough for everyday wear. Fine-quality ruby is extremely rare, and the color of the gem is most important to its value. The best color is a medium or medium dark vivid red or slightly purplish red. If the gem is too light or has too much purple or orange.

Peridot

     People believe that peridot hosts magical powers and heal properties to protect against nightmares and to bring the wearer power and influence. This gemstone comes in several color variations ranging from yellowish green to brown, but most consumers are attracted to the bright lime greens and olive greens.

Sapphire

     Sapphire has been popular since the Middle Ages and according to folklore, will protect your loved ones from envy and harm. Medieval clergy wore sapphires to symbolize heaven, while commoners thought the gem attracted heavenly blessings. Blue sapphires range from very light to very dark greenish or violetish blue, as well as various shades of pure blue. The most prized colors are a medium to medium dark blue or slightly violetish blue. Sapphire is a variety of the gem species corundum and occurs in all colors of the rainbow. Pink, purple, green, orange, or yellow corundum are known by their color (pink sapphire, green sapphire).

Opal

     The name opal derives from the Greek Opallos, meaning "to see a change (of color)." Opals range in color from milky white to black with flashes of yellow, orange, green, red, and blue. An opal's beauty is the product of contrast between its color play and its background. The black opal is the most valuable and are even more precious than diamonds.












November

Topaz

     Topaz is a gemstone available in a rich rainbow of colors. They come in yellow, pink, purple, orange, and the many popular blue tones. It is thought to be soothing, healing, stimulating and recharging.

Citrine

     Citrine, the other birthstone for November is known as the "healing quartz". This golden gemstone is said to support vitality and health while encouraging and guiding hope, energy and warmth within the wearer. Citrine can be found in a variety of shades ranging from pastel yellow to dark brownish orange. It is one of the most affordable of gemstones and plentiful in nature.


















December

Tanzanite

     tanzanite exhibits a rich violet-blue color for which the gemstone is treasured; often it is heat-treated to achieve this color. Colors range from blue to purple, and tanzanites that are medium dark in tone, vivid in saturation, and slightly violet blue command premium prices. As tanzanite can be less expensive than sapphire, it often was purchased as an alternative. However, it has increased in popularity and now is valued more for its own beauty and brilliance than as a sapphire substitute.

Zircon

     Zircon is found in a wide range of colors such as: blue, yellow, orange, brown, green, colorless, and red (the most prized color). For many years colorless zircon was used to imitate diamonds. Folk wisdom grants zircon the power to relieve pain, wet the appetite, protect travelers from disease and injury, to ensure a warm welcome, and to prevent nightmares guaranteeing a deep, tranquil sleep.

Turquoise

     Turquoise varies in color from greenish blue, through robin's egg-blue, to sky blue shades and its transparency ranges from translucent to opaque. Turquoise is plentiful and is available in a wide range of sizes. It is most often used for beads, cabochons, carvings, and inlays.
